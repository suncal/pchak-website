01 — Engineering Context How this world actually works

Anyone can sell you boxes. The craft is provenance, compliance, and headroom you don't regret.

Field Guide

Specify like an insider.

Three provables: authenticity (no counterfeit or relabeled gear), warranty (OEMs honor authorized-channel serials; gray-market units are routinely refused), and provenance (firmware and licensing that pass security review). For federal buyers there's a fourth: supply-chain documentation that procurement rules increasingly demand. The gray-market discount is real — you pay it back at the first failure or audit.

The Trade Agreements Act restricts many federal purchases to products made or 'substantially transformed' in designated countries. It's a country-of-origin test, enforced at the SKU level — the same model can exist in compliant and non-compliant builds. If your purchase touches a GSA schedule or most federal contracts, TAA status isn't a detail; it's eligibility. We verify it per line item.

Sum the realistic per-device draw (a Wi-Fi 7 AP can pull 30–50 W), apply the switch's total PoE budget — not just per-port class — and leave 20–30% headroom for the devices you haven't imagined yet. The classic failure is a switch with plenty of ports and an exhausted power budget: half the ports go dark for new APs.

Ratings, not adjectives: IP codes for dust and water, MIL-STD-810 methods for shock, vibration, and temperature — with the specific tested methods listed. Rugged costs 2–3× enterprise-grade; field replacement costs downtime, travel, and data gaps. The math usually says: rugged where devices live on vehicles, docks, and job sites; standard everywhere climate-controlled.

Ask for the lifecycle dates before the price. End-of-sale starts the countdown; end-of-support ends security patches — which for federal and regulated environments effectively ends the asset. We quote with published roadmap status and flag anything inside its final third of life, because a discount on dying hardware is not a discount.

Land-mobile radio remains the instant, infrastructure-light, push-to-talk backbone for field and safety operations — it works when cellular doesn't. Broadband adds data, video, and coverage depth. The modern answer is rarely either/or: it's LMR for voice-critical instants, broadband for everything data — engineered together, which is precisely the kind of mixed fleet our partner lineup exists to serve.
